## 01_THE_ATMOSPHERE

The smell of burning solder and sweat dominated the air in late May. My friend's living room was stuffed with oversized sofas and a TV that did not fit into the warehouse-turned-apartment aesthetic. 

The four of us crowded over the **Nano**—a piece of silicon the size of a matchbox. For hours, we took turns hopelessly trying to put three wires in their place. 

> **Current System Status:**
> - Location: Pune, MH.
> - Ambient Temp: 42°C (Estimated)
> - Confidence Level: CRITICAL_LOW

All we gained was bad breath, sweaty backs, and a thinning confidence. It was silent, heavy, and failing.

---

## 02_TECHNICAL_INTERVENTION

Silently, it was agreed that this task was a waste of time. Time that would have been better invested in playing the new *Naruto* game—the real reason we were invited over. 

Then **Greg** decided he’d had enough. He took charge.

Greg told us to hold the wires steady. We were happy to comply. His brow contracted—the same look he had when trying to break down a logic problem. He held the gun in one hand and the solder strip in the other. 

His hand was steady. We got our hopes up.

---

## 03_THE_FLICKER

The strip melted. The wire stuck to the first hole—the size of a needle's eye. 

**The battery connected.**

The tiny LED on the board lit up. It was a triumphant moment, however short-lived. I saw Greg's face relax, his eyes darting from the cell to the LED, already deciding on how to relish his win.

Then, the air changed. 

The sweaty smell was replaced by an **acrid tang**. A hint of flicker on the orange LED. 

### SYSTEM_ALERT: THERMAL_RUNAWAY
`[ ERROR ] : CRISP_WISPS_OF_SMOKE_DETECTED`
`[ ERROR ] : LITHIUM_CELL_VENTING`

---

## 04_KINETIC_RESPONSE

Four teenagers see smoke; we worked in perfect sync. 

1. **Yank:** One of us pulled the lead.
2. **Dispose:** Greg dumped the matchbox and broke the solder (I saw the wince tightening around his eyes).
3. **Ventilate:** I threw the windows open.

Our hopes for a working entry were successfully evaporating into the Pune heat. 

The silence that followed lasted as long as our success. Greg finally looked up, wiped a streak of soot from his thumb, and broke the tension:

> "So how about that Naruto game now huh?"
